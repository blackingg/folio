Last year, a friend reached out to me with an exciting proposition: a **WHO hackathon**. The mission was ambitious: building a modern banking application tailored for users in the Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC). I joined the team as a Frontend Engineer, ready to tackle the unique challenges of fintech in a developing market.

However, as many hackathon stories go, the reality didn't quite match the initial excitement.

#### The Reality Check: Building Without a Foundation

As we started, it became clear that we were missing critical pieces of the puzzle. Our team wasn't "assembled for success" from the jump.

1. **The Backend Void**: We didn't have a Backend Engineer. The plan was to "pick one up along the way" — a strategy that rarely works in the high-stakes environment of a hackathon. Without a dedicated engineer, our infrastructure was theoretical at best.
2. **Design Disconnect**: Our designer, while enthusiastic, struggled to meet the complex requirements of a fintech application. The gap between the vision and the hand-off was growing.
3. **A Shift in Focus**: Eventually, the lack of a clear path to a backend and the mounting technical debt led the team to step away from the project.

It would have been easy to walk away then. But I've always disliked leaving work unfinished. I saw the potential in what we were building — a financial tool that could actually make a difference.

#### Turning Setbacks into Stacks

I decided to take the frontend and push it to a functional, usable stage on my own. This wasn't just about finishing a project; it was about proving that I could adapt and build something premium even under real-world constraints.

I leaned into a modern mobile stack to bring the vision to life:

- **Expo & React Native**: For a high-performance, cross-platform experience.
- **NativeWind**: To implement a sleek, Tailwind-powered design system.
- **Zustand**: For lightweight and efficient state management.
- **Reanimated**: To add those subtle glassmorphism-inspired micro-animations that make an app feel premium.

#### Unexpected Wins: Beyond the Code

While the hackathon didn't end with a trophy, it gave me something far more valuable. My friend and I, who had joined the team together, found our friendship evolving into a professional partnership. Working through the chaos and the subsequent solo development allowed us to build a level of professional trust that goes beyond just "knowing each other." We learned how to problem-solve as a unit, and that synergy is now one of my most prized takeaways from the experience.

#### The Architecture: Frontend-First Engineering

When the backend team evaporated, I didn't just build static screens. I built a **Frontend-First** architecture that mocks real-world latency and state persistence.

One of the highlights is the **Bank State Management** built with **Zustand**. I chose Zustand over Redux because of its boilerplate-free simplicity, which was crucial for rapid solo development.

#### Deep Dive: The Feature Set

**1. Simulated API Latency (Account Resolution)**

In a real banking app, you don't just send money to a number; you verify the name. In `SendPage.tsx`, I implemented a simulated account resolution hook. When a user types 10 digits and selects a bank, the app triggers a `useEffect` that mimics an API call with a 1.5s delay. This allowed me to design and test the UX for loading states (`ActivityIndicator`) and error handling before a single backend endpoint even existed.

**2. Glassmorphism & UI System**

Fintech apps live and die by their "trust factor," which is often conveyed through design. I used **NativeWind** (Tailwind CSS for React Native) to build a consistent design system.

- **Glassmorphism**: Using `rgba` overlays and subtle borders to create depth.
- **Iconography**: Integrated `react-native-solar-icons` using the `bold-duotone` type to give the app that "premium" digital-bank feel.

**3. Complex Navigation Strategy**

The app uses a hybrid navigation model:

- **Bottom Tab Navigator**: For primary views (Home, Send, Invest, Cards).
- **Native Stack Navigator**: For "deep" actions like `AddMoneyPage`, ensuring smooth transitions.

#### Technical Challenges Overcome

- **Keyboard Management**: Implementing `Keyboard.dismiss()` on forms to ensure the "Confirm Transfer" button is always accessible and the UX feels "native."
- **Immutable State Updates**: Managing nested arrays (like the list of cards) in Zustand to ensure React properly re-renders when a user toggles a "Freeze Card" switch.
